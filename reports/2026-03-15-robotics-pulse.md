# Robotics Pulse — Mar 01 – Mar 15, 2026

## Top Signal
Physical AI is becoming the decisive competitive advantage in manufacturing, and the funding velocity confirms it. Rhoda AI's $450M exit from stealth to train robots from video, Mind Robotics' $500M raise from Rivian's founder, and Machina Labs' momentum at AW26 signal that the industry has moved past theoretical potential—factories now see AI-embodied systems as essential to solving labor constraints and innovation speed. This matters because it's no longer a robotics play; it's a manufacturing transformation play, and the winners will be companies that can deploy adaptable physical AI at scale, not just build better hardware.

## Developments

- **Rhoda AI exits stealth with $450M for video-based robot training** — The company's approach to training robots from real-world video footage rather than controlled lab data addresses the critical gap between demonstration and deployment. This capital injection signals investor conviction that video-to-behavior learning is the unlock for generalizable physical AI. (The Robot Report)

- **Mind Robotics raises $500M to automate Rivian's factory** — RJ Scaringe's new venture will train on and deploy within Rivian's production environment, creating a tight feedback loop between robotics development and manufacturing application. Insider access to factory data and deployment is a structural advantage that other industrial robotics startups lack. (TechCrunch)

- **Oxa closes Series D for configurable autonomous vehicle software** — The company's platform works across 20+ vehicle types, solving the customization problem that has historically fragmented industrial mobility automation. This modularity-first approach could accelerate deployment across logistics, mining, and warehouse operations. (The Robot Report)

- **Data security emerges as foundational trust issue for physical AI** — ANYbotics' CEO frames cyber and data security as non-negotiable for field robots operating in critical infrastructure. As robots move into mission-critical roles, security vulnerabilities become operational vulnerabilities—this will reshape vendor selection criteria for enterprises. (The Robot Report)

- **Anduril reaches $60B valuation in defense robotics race** — The autonomous systems firm's doubling in valuation within a year reflects accelerating defense spending on AI-powered robotics and spatial intelligence for autonomous operations. The gap between civilian and defense robotics investment continues to widen. (TechCrunch)

## Figure Watch
Figure AI's humanoid robots are demonstrating autonomous real-world capability in unstructured environments (living room cleaning), while partnerships with automotive manufacturers like BMW signal industrial adoption pathways. These aren't lab demos—they're moving toward deployment-ready systems in controlled commercial settings.

## Trend Line
Capital is aggressively flowing toward physical AI systems that can learn from real-world data and deploy across multiple platforms, while enterprise buyers (manufacturers, logistics, defense) are shifting from "will robots work?" to "how fast can we integrate them?"—and security/data governance have jumped from secondary concerns to primary vendor requirements.
