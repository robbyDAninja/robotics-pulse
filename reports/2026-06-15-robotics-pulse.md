# Robotics Pulse — Jun 01 – Jun 15, 2026

## Top Signal
Autonomous freight is crossing the commercialization threshold with two major plays: Einride's SPAC debut and Gatik's PepsiCo partnership signal that the long-haul and regional logistics problems are finally being solved at scale. These aren't pilot programs—they're Fortune 500 deployments with real revenue. This matters because logistics is the highest-ROI robotics vertical, and success here funds everything else in the industry. ([The Robot Report, Jun 15](https://www.therobotreport.com/autonomous-freight-developer-einride-goes-public-via-spac/); [The Robot Report, Jun 12](https://www.therobotreport.com/gatik-brings-autonomous-freight-pepsico-north-american-supply-chain/))

## Developments

- **Modular over humanoid: Theker raises $85M for reconfigurable factory robots** — The company is building machines designed to swap configurations rather than be locked into a single form factor, directly challenging the humanoid-first design philosophy dominating the space. This approach reduces re-engineering costs and increases deployment flexibility across different tasks, suggesting the market is learning that morphology-agnostic design wins long-term. ([TechCrunch, Jun 12](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/11/theker-just-raised-85m-to-build-the-factory-robot-that-doesnt-specialize-in-anything/))

- **Contact-rich manipulation emerges as the frontier** — Research presented at ICRA 2026 and captured in multiple arXiv papers shows the field is converging on tactile sensing and contact dynamics as the missing piece for dexterous work. ContactWorld benchmarks and new acoustic waveguide tactile sensors represent a shift from vision-only to multimodal perception, directly enabling the insertion and assembly tasks that have resisted automation. ([IEEE Spectrum, Jun 9](https://spectrum.ieee.org/agilink-contact-intelligence-robot-manipulation); arXiv robotics papers)

- **World models for real-world physics gain traction** — Multiple new preprints (μ₀, FlowMo-WM, domain-adaptive policy learning) tackle the sim-to-real gap by modeling momentum, drift, and non-stationary disturbances. These aren't academic exercises—they're solving the problem that keeps robots failing on real hardware, with practical applications to quadrotors and aquatic vehicles. ([arXiv, Jun 15](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.13769))

- **Edge AI makes robotics accessible beyond engineers** — Hello Robot's WEF recognition and the emphasis on Edge AI tools suggest robotics is entering the "Windows moment"—commoditized, abstracted intelligence that non-specialists can deploy. This mirrors the PC revolution and removes a major adoption blocker for mid-market manufacturers. ([The Robot Report, Jun 11](https://www.therobotreport.com/hello-robot-is-recognized-by-world-economic-forum-as-a-tech-pioneer/); [The Robot Report, Jun 13](https://www.therobotreport.com/computers-software-windows-utility-robots/))

- **Burro ships 1M+ hours of outdoor autonomy data** — The Grande 44's deployment record provides rare ground truth for industrial towing and logistics, addressing the chronic data scarcity that constrains reinforcement learning in the real world. Open datasets like XRZero-G0 are beginning to fix the training bottleneck that has plagued the industry. ([The Robot Report, Jun 15](https://www.therobotreport.com/burro-introduces-grande-44-with-proven-outdoor-autonomy-built-for-heavy-industry/); [The Robot Report, Jun 11](https://www.therobotreport.com/inside-xrzero-g0-a-new-2000-hour-open-dataset-for-robotics-research/))

## Figure Watch
No significant Figure AI news this cycle.

## Trend Line
The industry is shifting from morphology wars (humanoid vs. legged vs. wheeled) to capability specialization: autonomous freight is commercializing at scale, contact intelligence is replacing vision-only approaches, and edge AI is democratizing deployment—meaning the winners will be those solving specific logistics/manipulation problems, not those chasing general-purpose form factors.
