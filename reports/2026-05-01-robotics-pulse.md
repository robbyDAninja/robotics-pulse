# Robotics Pulse — Apr 17 – May 01, 2026

## Top Signal
Tactile sensing has moved from research curiosity to production necessity. Multiple companies (XELA Robotics, DAIMON Robotics, optical tactile simulation frameworks) are shipping and refining contact-aware systems, while a wave of arXiv papers on tactile-aware loco-manipulation and haptic feedback for surgical robots confirms the field has solved the "why" and is now racing on the "how." For AI agent builders, this is the inflection point: robots that can reliably manipulate deformable materials, sense contact uncertainty, and adapt to real-world friction are no longer prototypes—they're entering factories and surgical suites. (Sources: The Robot Report, arXiv Robotics, IEEE Spectrum)

## Developments

- **Deformable materials emerge as the practical frontier for physical AI** — Createme's CEO frames fabric, textiles, and other deformable materials as the true test of physical AI capability, positioning this as the next battleground after rigid-object bin picking. This matters because it's a market segment with real volume (apparel, soft goods assembly) and high automation resistance; companies solving this get a defensible moat. (The Robot Report)

- **Humanoid deployment accelerates with talent and capital influx** — Schaeffler committed to deploying 1,000 Hexagon humanoids by 2032, while Apptronik hired Waymo veteran Daniel Chu as Chief Product Officer to drive commercialization of its $935M-funded platform. These aren't concept announcements; they're production timelines backed by automotive supply-chain credibility and autonomous vehicle operational expertise. (The Robot Report)

- **Manufacturing language models democratize automation design** — Launchpad Build AI released a Manufacturing Language Model that accepts photos, videos, or CAD as input to accelerate industrial automation design for high-mix, low-volume production. This lowers the barrier to deploying custom robot workflows outside tier-one factories, directly addressing a bottleneck in physical AI adoption. (The Robot Report)

- **California opens autonomous truck licensing; regulatory arbitrage closes** — California's DMV expanded safety and oversight frameworks to permit autonomous trucking, closing a regulatory gap that existed for robotaxis. This removes a major blocker for last-mile autonomy and signals state-level acceptance of operational robotics in transport. (The Robot Report)

- **Flex–Teradyne partnership scales physical AI across supply chains** — The expanded partnership targets global manufacturing facilities, combining Teradyne's AI robotics revenue growth ($91M Q1 2026) with Flex's manufacturing footprint. This signals that physical AI is moving from pilots into standardized deployment playbooks. (The Robot Report)

## Figure Watch
Figure AI continues ramping Figure 03 production and secured visibility at high-profile events (Melania Trump event), positioning the company as the consumer-facing humanoid narrative leader. No major funding or technical breakthrough announcements, but production scaling is the quietest validation a hardware startup can get.

## Trend Line
The industry is shifting from "can robots do X?" to "how do we deploy X at scale?"—tactile sensing, deformable material handling, and long-horizon planning are graduating from research to integration, while regulatory and partnership frameworks are removing the non-technical barriers that have historically stalled commercialization.
