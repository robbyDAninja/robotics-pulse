# Robotics Pulse — Mar 18 – Apr 01, 2026

## Top Signal

The humanoid robot sector has crossed into mass production and enterprise integration. AGIBOT's rollout of its 10,000th unit, combined with Humanoid's live warehouse logistics proof-of-concept with SAP, and Figure AI's White House appearance backing a 100K robot plan, signal that humanoids are transitioning from prototype theater to actual deployment at scale. This matters because it validates the business model: companies are now racing to integrate agentic AI into embodied systems for real logistics work, not just demos. The bottleneck is shifting from "can we build them?" to "can we train, deploy, and keep them working reliably across dozens of sites?"

## Developments

- **Autonomous route learning removes manual training friction** — Brain Corp's BrainOS Clean 2.0 introduces SelfPath AI, enabling Tennant cleaning robots to autonomously map and adapt routes without manual programming or training data labeling. This matters because training data collection has been a hidden cost and scaling bottleneck in commercial robotics; automation of that step directly improves unit economics. ([The Robot Report][1])

- **Defense and maritime robotics attract massive capital** — Shield AI raised $2B for autonomous defense drones (acquiring Aechelon for simulation capabilities), Saronic raised $1.75B for autonomous ships, and PDW raised $110M for multi-mission drones. The concentration of 8-figure+ rounds in autonomous systems suggests capital is moving away from narrow-use-case robots toward platform plays in high-stakes domains. ([The Robot Report][14], [15], [20])

- **Data infrastructure becomes strategic** — Nomadic raised $8.4M to structure and index footage from autonomous vehicles into searchable datasets, addressing the bottleneck of making robot sensor data useful for model training at scale. This sits at the intersection of robotics and AI development: you can't improve models without clean, labeled data from deployed systems. ([TechCrunch][17])

- **Strategic partnerships over vertical integration** — FedEx explicitly chose partnerships with external vendors (e.g., Berkshire Gray) rather than building proprietary automation in-house, while multiple robotics startups are partnering with Google DeepMind to integrate foundation models. This signals a consolidation around modular, integrable systems rather than closed ecosystems. ([TechCrunch][16]; [TechCrunch][41])

- **Humanoid data collection is becoming a gig economy** — MIT reported that workers globally (including medical students in Nigeria) are being hired at scale to record teleoperation data for humanoid training, creating a new labor arbitrage model for generating synthetic data. This matters because it's cheaper than hiring roboticists and speeds training cycles, but raises questions about data quality and labor practices. ([MIT Technology Review][2], [4])

## Figure Watch

Figure AI appears positioned as the public face of humanoid commercialization. The company's White House appearance with Melania Trump, backed by announced support from Nvidia and OpenAI for a 100K robot production plan, positions the startup as the visible leader in the space—though the company is simultaneously expanding horizontally through a new venture (Hark) to build a "family of AI devices." Founder Brett Adcock's valuation has reportedly doubled to $5.6B in four months, driven by both media visibility and tangible production milestones.

## Trend Line

Capital is consolidating around platform robotics (humanoids, autonomous vehicles, drones) and their enabling infrastructure (simulation, data management, foundation models), while point solutions and single-use robots are becoming acquisition targets rather than standalone investments.
